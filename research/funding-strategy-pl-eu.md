## 1. The play (TL;DR)

1. **Incorporate the sp. z o.o. now** — but for the _right_ reasons: the BGK
   dual-use loan's 12-month clock is already lost for round 1 (§8.1), what the
   incorporation actually unlocks is the **October–December 2026 window**: the
   next Ścieżka SMART single-SME round (apps **2026-10-29 → 2026-12-29**,
   500M PLN) and Scale Up Green (–2026-09-30).
2. **The voivodeship decision is the one irreversible lever** — Polska
   Wschodnia registration unlocks the only pre-incorporation→600k PLN grant
   ladder (Platformy Startowe I→IIa), a 70% aid intensity (50% base + 20pp
   small), and the fattest regional loan envelopes. Registering in Warsaw
   city prices all regional aid at 0%. Decide this before signing the KRS
   papers, §2.2.
3. **Cheapest money first:** ulga B+R with the art. 18da **cash refund**
   works even pre-profit (it is the only tax instrument that pays a
   loss-making startup real cash), IP Box 5% waits ready for revenue, and a
   BGK de-minimis guarantee makes a small bank loan bankable without
   collateral. Tax instruments cost paperwork, not equity. §3.2.
4. **2026 is late-cycle for the 2021–2027 EU money** — peak spending year,
   KPO largely contracted, GBER's current text runs to end-2026. The window
   for "easy" regional + FENG applications is now through ~2027, not
   indefinitely. (ESTIMATE, §4.1/§8.7)
5. Target stack for the first 18 months (worked in §5): **600k PLN (IIa,
   if PW) or ~1–2M PLN SMART R&D module (anywhere) + 400k PLN Scale Up
   Green + B+R cash refund + guarantee-backed bridge** — all non-dilutive,
   all compatible if the de-minimis and GBER ledgers are kept clean (§4.2).

## 2. Structural decisions that gate funding

### 2.1 Incorporation timing — what the clocks actually say

- **Do it now, but with corrected expectations.** The catalog's strongest
  argument (start the BGK WPPZ 12-month clock) no longer buys round 1: WPPZ
  applications run 2026-10-29 → 2027-01-28 and require **KRS registration
  ≥12 months before applying** — a company registered in August 2026 is
  eligible for a _second_ round at the earliest (none announced). (FACT for
  the dates/requirement per catalog §2.4; the round-1 impossibility is
  arithmetic.) The clock still matters: start it anyway for whatever 2027–28
  rounds follow.
- What incorporation _does_ unlock immediately: SMART 2026/2 (announce
  2026-09-29), Scale Up Green (–2026-09-30), Granty na Eurogranty
  (–2026-09-03), tax reliefs from day one, SUDOP/de-minimis identity, and —
  if PW — the Platformy Startowe path (Hub of Talents 3 cutoff 2026-09-30,
  catalog §1.1).
- Practitioner caveat: many regional calls expect **~12 months of operating
  history** (with startup exceptions); national FENG/FEPW startup
  instruments do not. (ESTIMATE, grantbot.pl practitioner guide.) Every
  month of delay pushes the "eligible for the track-record-gated calls"
  date right.

### 2.2 Voivodeship = a funding decision (the one irreversible lever)

Two coherent routes; pick deliberately, don't drift into one:

**Route A — Polska Wschodnia registration** (lubelskie, podkarpackie,
podlaskie, świętokrzyskie, warmińsko-mazurskie):

- Unlocks **FEPW**: Platformy Startowe Komponent I incubation (the only
  pre-incorporation entry in the whole system) → Komponent IIa **600,000 PLN
  grant** (round VI apps –2026-09-17; next platform cutoff Hub of Talents 3
  Białystok/Łomża/Suwałki –2026-09-30, metal/machinery profile — a natural
  fit). (FACT, catalog §1.1–1.2.)
- Regional aid intensity **50% base + 20pp small = 70%** on investment aid —
  the ceiling that scales every CAPEX grant, the PSI CIT exemption, and the
  BGK premium instruments. (FACT — parp.gov.pl aid-map page, verbatim;
  legal basis Dz.U. 2021 poz. 2422; re-verified 2026-07-21.)
- A June 2025 amendment (Dz.U. 2025 poz. 908, in force 2025-07-08) adds
  **+10pp for STEP** (Strategic Technologies for Europe Platform) investments
  in the eastern 107(3)(a) regions. Additive-manufacturing capacity can
  plausibly be framed as STEP-relevant — worth a professional check when a
  CAPEX application is actually drafted. (FACT that the amendment exists;
  ESTIMATE on applicability to us.)
- PSI quality-criteria threshold is _lower_ in 50% regions — 4 points vs 6
  elsewhere, out of 13 for production projects (§2.4). (FACT — PSI investor
  handbook, read directly 2026-07-21.)
- Fattest regional loan envelopes: podkarpackie >780M PLN, lubelskie
  ~316.8M PLN via BGK trust funds (§3.4). (FACT — BGK/LAWP announcements.)
- FEPW extras: Wzornictwo w MŚP (design), GOZ business-model grants
  (catalog §1.7). (FACT existence.)
- Costs of Route A: talent pool, logistics, and the practical requirement to
  _actually operate_ there (registered office + real activity; PSI/decyzja
  requires the investment physically in-region). Also the Platformy path
  forces the incubation program's timeline and platform choice.

**Route B — register where you already are** (e.g., Warsaw or Kraków):

- Everything national/EU still works: SMART, Scale Up Green, Eurogranty,
  EIC, Eurostars, BGK guarantees, all tax reliefs except location-gated aid.
- Warsaw city + adjacent powiaty: **0% regional aid** — no PSI CIT
  exemption, 0% BGK technology-premium instruments, no regional CAPEX
  grants. Mazowiecki regionalny (outside the capital ring) reaches up to
  40%; selected warszawski-wschodni/zachodni gminy 25–35%. (FACT for the
  0%/map structure — parp.gov.pl + Dz.U. 2021 poz. 2422; gmina detail via
  grantera, SECONDARY.)
- Małopolskie contrast: 40% base + 20pp small = 60% (FACT — parp.gov.pl
  verbatim "małopolskie – 40%", re-verified 2026-07-21); pipeline
  includes the SME-friendly **bony na innowacje** (innovation vouchers for
  buying R&D services — last edition 3.6× oversubscribed, 119 applications,
  resolved 2025-12-23; check the 2026/27 harmonogram for the next one).
  (FACT for the voucher call stats — fundusze.malopolska.pl.) Kraków also
  hosts FORT KRAKÓW (DIANA Poland) — relevant only to the defence option.

**Recommendation:** Route A **only if** you are genuinely willing to build
the production site in the east — the 600k IIa ladder + 70% intensity +
4/10 PSI threshold is the single largest non-dilutive package available to
a fresh company, and it is location-locked. If the honest answer is "the
company will live where I live," take Route B, skip the FEPW ladder without
regret, and lean on SMART + Scale Up Green + tax stack — those are
location-agnostic. A Warsaw-city registered office specifically is the one
strictly dominated choice (0% band) if any CAPEX aid is ever wanted;
even moving the registered office one ring out changes the map position.

### 2.3 Legal form and tax regime — one active trap

- sp. z o.o. as planned; S24 registration is days, not weeks. (Context.)
- **Do not elect estoński CIT.** It looks attractive (10% for small
  taxpayers) but a company on ryczałt od dochodów spółek **cannot use ulga
  B+R or IP Box** (excluded by regime), and grant inflows under estoński
  create their own taxable-income complications. Classic CIT (9% reduced
  rate for small taxpayers anyway) + B+R + IP Box beats it for an
  R&D-heavy company. (FACT on the exclusions — leinonen/ggi, SECONDARY but
  uncontested; the 9% small-CIT rate is standard law.) ★
- Register the PKD codes to include both software (62.01) and manufacturing
  (25.xx/32.xx) from day one — several instruments screen by declared
  activity. (ESTIMATE, practitioner hygiene.)

### 2.4 Polska Strefa Inwestycji (decyzja o wsparciu) — cheap option, take it seriously at CAPEX time

- CIT exemption as regional aid on a _new investment_, worth up to the aid
  map intensity (→ up to 70% of qualifying CAPEX in PW as a tax shield);
  decyzja o wsparciu issued for **12, 14 or 15 years** by region. (FACT —
  PSI investor handbook (UWM/WMSSE/SSSE, Olsztyn 2025, hosted by PAIH),
  read directly 2026-07-21.)
- Minimum qualifying costs, micro column of the county-unemployment table:
  **2M PLN (lowest-unemployment counties) down to 0.2M PLN** (>250% of
  average unemployment, SOR cities and adjacent gminy, and a named list of
  eastern powiaty incl. białostocki, suwalski, chełmski) — i.e. a micro
  company's entry ticket is 200k–2M PLN by location, and a Fuse-class SLS
  pilot (~250k PLN) can already clear the bar in favorable counties.
  (FACT — handbook threshold table, read directly; the R&D/modern-services
  95% reduction applies to large/medium.)
- Quality criteria: **4 / 5 / 6 points by location** (lower threshold in
  higher-intensity regions), out of **13 for production projects** (8
  economic + 5 social; 12 for services), with points required from both
  groups. Micro/small status itself scores 1 point, as do "prowadzenie
  działalności B+R" and "robotyzacja i automatyzacja" — 4 points in a 50%
  region is very reachable for this company. Qualifying costs may
  alternatively be **2-year gross labor costs of newly hired staff**
  instead of CAPEX. (FACT — handbook criteria tables, read directly.)
- Action: this is not a now-decision; it is a **before-buying-machines**
  decision. Apply _before_ starting the investment (incentive effect —
  aid must be sought before project start, GBER art. 6). (FACT.)

### 2.5 Run a de-minimis ledger from day one

- The ceiling is **€300,000 per "single undertaking" over any rolling
  3-year window, counted day-to-day**, in gross-grant-equivalent (EDB),
  under Reg. (EU) 2023/2831 (in force since 2024-01-01). (FACT — UOKiK
  zasady-pomocy-de-minimis, verbatim extraction.)
- Everything de-minimis draws on it: 100%-intensity startup grants (Scale
  Up Green–type), BGK de-minimis guarantees (their EDB, not face value),
  many small regional instruments. GBER-based aid (SMART co-financing, PSI,
  regional CAPEX grants) does **not** consume it but has its own intensity
  caps. (FACT for the mechanics; instrument-by-instrument legal basis is
  stated in each call's regulations — always check which regime a call
  runs under.)
- Check your own ledger anytime at **sudop.uokik.gov.pl** (search by NIP;
  grantors must report within 7 days via SHRIMP). (FACT — UOKiK.)
- Guarantee safe-harbor: a guarantee ≤ €2.25M and ≤5 years counts within
  the ceiling under the regulation's simplified rule; BGK certifies the
  exact EDB in a zaświadczenie at grant. (FACT — Reg. 2023/2831 art. 4 via
  EUR-Lex search extraction + BGK terms document dated 2026-04-16.)

### 3.2 Tax instruments (NEW layer — the quiet money)

Poland's CIT toolkit, in the order it becomes real cash for us:

**a. Ulga B+R (art. 18d) + the art. 18da startup cash refund — start
day one.**

- Deduction: **200% of R&D personnel costs** (salaries + employer
  contributions), 100% extra for other qualified costs (materials,
  equipment depreciation, expert opinions); 200% of everything only with
  CBR status. 6-year carry-forward. (FACT — podatki.gov.pl ulga B+R page,
  re-verified 2026-07-21.)
- Loss-year treatment is the startup killer-feature: normally a 6-year
  carry-forward, **but art. 18da pays cash**: in the first tax year (or the
  second, if then still micro/small/medium) a loss-making company receives
  **unused deduction × the CIT rate** as a refund via CIT/BR. Taking the
  refund forfeits the carry-forward for those amounts. (FACT — lexlege
  consolidated art. 18da + podatki.gov.pl, re-verified 2026-07-21.)
- **Keep the founding clean to preserve the refund:** art. 18da(4) denies
  it to companies created by transformation/merger/division, converted
  sole proprietorships, or capitalized with a pre-existing enterprise or
  in-kind assets worth >€10k. A plain cash-founded sp. z o.o. qualifies;
  an aport of existing business assets kills it. (FACT — art. 18da(4),
  re-verified 2026-07-21.)
- Effective yield: up to ~38% of R&D payroll back at the 19% rate
  (Grant Thornton framing; at the 9% small-taxpayer rate the refund is
  proportionally smaller — model both, ESTIMATE on which rate applies to
  the refund arithmetic — verify with an advisor).
- **Stacking rule with grants:** costs reimbursed by an EU/PARP grant are
  **excluded** from the relief base — only the own-funded share counts.
  Mixed projects need phase/cost allocation discipline from the first
  invoice. (FACT — TPA 2026 practitioner analysis of ruling practice,
  SECONDARY but standard reading of art. 18d ust. 5.)
- Practical: qualifying R&D = developing the pricing/geometry engine, mesh
  processing, new process development (SLS parameter work). Keep a project
  time-registry from day one; the ewidencja is what the KIS asks for.

**b. IP Box (art. 24d) — 5% on platform income, ready when revenue is.**

- Self-developed software is a qualified IP ("autorskie prawo do programu
  komputerowego"); the 2019 MF objaśnienia are the operative guidance;
  per-IP ewidencja (art. 24e) + nexus ratio required. (FACT —
  podatki.gov.pl objaśnienia.)
- Post-Polski-Ład you can use **B+R and IP Box simultaneously** — art. 24d
  ust. 9a lets B+R costs be deducted from the 5%-taxed IP income (scope of
  which costs is disputed in KIS practice — PwC; plan conservatively).
  (FACT for the rule — gov.pl; SECONDARY for the dispute nuance.)
- A 2026 tightening (min. 3 FTE condition) was proposed but **not
  enacted** as of the sources' Feb 2026 review. Watch. (ESTIMATE —
  taxsummaries.)
- Fit note: IP Box taxes _IP income_. For a parts-manufacturing revenue mix
  the qualified stream is the platform/licensing share, not the printed
  part — the practical yield depends on how revenue is structured. Get a
  ruling (interpretacja indywidualna) before relying. (ESTIMATE.)

**c. Ulga na robotyzację (art. 38eb) — probably not ours; know why.**

- 50% extra deduction on industrial-robot costs via depreciation, **only
  for FY2022–2026**; the extension bill was negatively assessed by the
  Council of Ministers 2026-03-06 — the relief dies with FY2026. (FACT —
  prawo.pl.) A 2026 WSA ruling lets depreciation on robots _acquired by
  end-2026_ keep the benefit after 2026. (FACT — prawo.pl.)
- Whether a 3D printer is an "industrial robot" (automatically controlled,
  programmable, multipurpose, ≥3 DOF, manipulative/locomotive, sensor-linked)
  is exactly the contested definitional question; KIS reads it restrictively,
  WSA I SA/Po 167/25 pushed back. (FACT that the dispute exists — KPMG +
  PARP note.) Combined with the deduction being income-capped (worthless
  pre-profit) and the sunset: **de-prioritize**; revisit only if profitable
  and buying AM cells before year-end 2026.

**d. Ulga na prototyp (30% of trial-production/launch costs, capped at 10%
of annual income) and ulga na ekspansję (extra deduction up to 1M PLN/yr,
conditional on 2-year revenue growth)** — both income-gated, so near-zero
value pre-profit, but costs carry forward (6 years). Log qualifying costs
now (first SLS production runs = classic trial-production), claim later.
(FACT rates — Grant Thornton/PwC taxsummaries, SECONDARY.)

**e. PSI CIT exemption** — see §2.4. The long-horizon heavyweight: at the
MJF-cell stage (~€660k CAPEX) a PW decyzja o wsparciu can shield ~70% of
the investment value in future CIT. (Arithmetic on FACT intensities.)

### 3.3 Soft loans & guarantees (BGK et al.)

- **Gwarancja de minimis** — up to 80% guarantee on working-capital or
  investment credit; free-ish (0.5% p.a. commission historically); the EDB
  consumes the €300k ledger. Current terms document dated 2026-04-16 on
  bgk.pl. (FACT existence/terms doc ★; commission ESTIMATE.) This is what
  makes a fresh sp. z o.o. bankable for the bridge financing in §4.3.
- **Biznesmax Plus / Ekomax** — guarantee + interest-subsidy variants for
  innovation/eco investments (catalog §2.5). Continuous, via banks.
- **Regional loan funds (BGK trust funds per voivodeship)** — pożyczka
  rozwojowa/inwestycyjna/płynnościowa via regional financing partners; up
  to 12-month grace; some products carry **partial forgiveness up to 25%**
  (a grant in loan's clothing — worth hunting). Envelopes: podkarpackie
  > 780M PLN; lubelskie ~316.8M PLN (269M EU + 47M national, 6 partner
  > institutions, incl. a forgiveness variant). (FACT — BGK/LAWP pages.) ★
- **Kredyt technologiczny (2.32): dormant** — allocation exhausted after
  the single 2023 round (88 projects, 763.6M PLN); BGK: no new round unless
  the budget is topped up; contact fkt@bgk.pl for updates. (FACT — BGK
  product page.) Its successor-in-spirit for us is WPPZ 2.33 (defence
  §7) and, at CAPEX time, regional investment grants + PSI.

## 4. How to win — economics and mechanics (NEW)

### 4.1 Aid-intensity arithmetic (what % you actually get)

- **R&D aid (GBER art. 25):** industrial research 50% base / experimental
  development 25% base; **+20pp small** (micro counts as small), +10pp
  medium; +15pp collaboration/wide-dissemination bonus; cap 80%. So a
  micro sp. z o.o. alone: **70% IR / 45% ED**, up to 80% IR with the
  dissemination bonus. Project classification into IR vs ED is where
  budgets are won and lost. (FACT — GBER via ISAP-published scheme
  regulation, incorporation-by-reference extraction; EUR-Lex itself is not
  fetchable from this tooling, so the exact art. 25(5)–(6) text was not
  re-read — the rates match standard GBER practice.) ★
- **Regional investment aid (GBER art. 14):** the 2022–2027 map — PW 50%,
  most others 25–40%, Warsaw ring 0% — **+20pp small**, +10pp medium;
  2025 amendment adds +10pp (east) / +5pp (west) for STEP investments.
  (FACT — parp.gov.pl + Dz.U. 2021 poz. 2422 + Dz.U. 2025 poz. 908.)
- **De-minimis-based instruments** ignore intensities (can be 100%) but
  consume the €300k ledger (§2.5).
- The **own contribution** is the complement of the intensity: a 70% IR
  project still needs 30% real money, and VAT is eligible **only if
  non-recoverable** — for a VAT-registered company, effectively never.
  Budget VAT as pure cash-flow. (FACT — wytyczne kwalifikowalności
  2021–2027, version 2025-03-14.)
- GBER's current text runs to 2026-12-31 with an adjustment period into
  2027 (dynamic references in the Polish scheme regulations); the
  post-2026 state-aid framework is not yet settled — one more reason the

### 4.2 De minimis mechanics — run the ledger

Covered in §2.5. Planning rule: reserve the €300k for instruments that
_must_ be de minimis (100%-intensity startup grants, guarantees); never
burn it on aid that could run under GBER instead. Before every application,
pull the SUDOP report and reconcile.

### 4.3 Cash-flow reality: zaliczka vs refundacja + the bridge

The non-dilutive trap is timing, not amount:

- **SMART/FENG advances (official PARP settlement rules):** total zaliczka
  up to **90% of the grant**, single tranche ≤40%, next tranche only after
  documenting spend of ≥70% of advances received; dedicated bank account;
  wniosek o płatność at least every 6 months (in practice quarterly).
  Refundacja covers the rest after approval by the intermediary
  institution. (FACT — PARP training deck "Rozliczanie SMART".) ★
- Practitioner reality: payment-claim verification takes weeks-to-months;
  plan 2–4 months of costs pre-financed at any time even with advances.
  (ESTIMATE — Crido/ECDF practitioner guides.)
- **Bridge:** a guarantee-backed working-capital line (§3.3) sized to ~1
  quarter of project burn is the standard answer; set it up when the grant
  agreement is signed, not when the first refund is late. (ESTIMATE.)
- 100%-intensity instruments (Scale Up Green, IIa) are paid through
  accelerator/platform operators — lighter mechanics, smaller amounts.

### 4.4 Procurement compliance (where beneficiaries bleed money)

- **Zasada konkurencyjności threshold is now 80,000 PLN net** (raised from
  50k by the 2025-03-14 amendment of the wytyczne, in force 2025-03-25;
  the 20–50k market-research duty was dropped). Above it: publish the
  inquiry in **Baza Konkurencyjności**, collect offers, document
  selection. (FACT — funduszeuedlamazowsza summary, verbatim "z 50 000 zł
  netto do 80 000 zł netto", re-verified 2026-07-21 + ministry guidelines
  page.)
- Violations cost via the **taryfikator** — percentage financial
  corrections (korekty) per the dedicated regulation; classic failures:
  splitting orders to duck the threshold, too-short offer windows,
  conflict-of-interest with the supplier. (FACT existence — ministry
  regulation page.)
- Startup-relevant: buying the SLS cell inside a grant project will exceed
  80k → full procedure. Plan 4–6 weeks for the procurement cycle.
